Pilot Essentials: Safety & Compliance

Technical clarity in flight starts with a deep understanding of the environment. Master the etiquette, legal requirements, and tools needed to fly with precision and professionalism.

Flight Etiquette & Safety

group_off

Maintain Separation

Never fly directly over unprotected people, moving vehicles, or public events. Maintaining a safe horizontal distance (minimum 30ft) is the hallmark of a professional pilot. Avoid the "cowboy" aesthetic; focus on disciplined, predictable flight paths.

height

The 400ft Ceiling

The legal altitude limit in most regions is 400ft (120m) above ground level. This ensures you remain clear of manned aircraft flight paths.

visibility

Visual Line of Sight

Even when flying FPV, a visual observer is often required to maintain VLOS with the craft to identify potential hazards.

Respecting Privacy

FPV drones are equipped with cameras that can be intrusive. Respect private property boundaries and avoid lingering near windows or backyards. Clarity in intent builds community trust.

privacy_tip

Finding Your Training Grounds

Knowing where to fly is as important as knowing how to fly. Use professional tools to identify legal airspace and community-approved spots.

map

B4UFLY & LAANC

The essential toolkit for US pilots. Use B4UFLY to check for temporary flight restrictions (TFRs) and controlled airspace. Use LAANC-enabled apps for near-instant authorization to fly in controlled zones.
